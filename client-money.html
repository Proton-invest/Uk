Client Money Handling Procedure

At TrustVest Capital, we are committed to protecting client funds and complying fully with applicable regulations. This document outlines how we manage, segregate, and protect client money.

1. Segregation of Client Funds

All client money is held in segregated client accounts, separate from TrustVest Capital’s own funds. These accounts are maintained with reputable, regulated financial institutions.

2. Client Money Bank Accounts

  • Accounts are designated specifically for client funds.
  • We perform regular reconciliations to ensure accuracy and compliance.
  • Banks holding client funds are selected based on their creditworthiness and regulatory status.

3. Use of Client Funds

Client money is never used for any purpose other than for the client's intended transaction. TrustVest Capital will not use client money to meet its own obligations or the obligations of other clients.

4. Withdrawals and Transfers

  • Withdrawals from client accounts are only made in accordance with the client’s instructions or for purposes allowed under applicable regulations.
  • Funds may be transferred between client accounts only when necessary and with proper documentation.

5. Reconciliation and Record-Keeping

  • We carry out daily reconciliations between internal records and bank balances.
  • All client transactions are recorded accurately and retained for audit and compliance purposes.

6. Client Money Protection

In the unlikely event of insolvency, segregated client funds remain protected and would not form part of TrustVest Capital’s assets. Additional safeguards may apply depending on regulatory jurisdiction.

7. Ongoing Oversight

Our compliance and finance teams monitor client money procedures regularly to ensure ongoing compliance with all legal and regulatory obligations.
